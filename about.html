Discover the enchanting history and culture of our island paradise

Brief history

Siquijor, known as the "Mystic Island," is a small province in the Central Visayas region of the Philippines. Located in the Bohol Sea, it is the third smallest province in the country. With its pristine beaches, lush forests, and rich cultural heritage, Siquijor offers a unique blend of natural beauty and mystical allure.

Legend tells of a "King Kihod" as the source of the island's name. Another version suggests it came from the Spanish word "Sequor," meaning "to follow the waves." Discovered by Esteban Rodriguez and Juan Aguirre of the Legaspi Expedition in 1565, Siquijor was known to Spanish conquistadors as "Isla de Fuego" or the "Island of Fire" due to the swarms of fireflies that lit up the island at night.

Municipalities of Siquijor

Siquijor

The capital town, located southwest of the island. Known for its annual Dilaab Festival or Fire Festival.

San Juan

The tourism center of the island, featuring diverse natural attractions and the Bugwas Festival.

Lazi

Known as the Music Town, home to the Saging (Banana) Festival and rich musical heritage.

Maria

Celebrates the Lubi Festival, showcasing various coconut products and uses.

Enrique Villanueva

Known for its Pamukad fish-harvesting activity and native delicacies.

Larena

Home to the province's major port and the Cano-an Festival featuring local culinary traditions.
